## The Lead Decay Curve: What Happens in the First 30 Minutes

The most comprehensive study in speed-to-lead history — MIT and InsideSales.com's analysis of over 55 million sales activities across 400+ companies — revealed a brutal decay curve that looks like this:

| Time After Lead Arrives | Contact Potential | Qualification Likelihood |
|------------------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| 1 minute | 100% (baseline) | 21× vs. 30 min |
| 5 minutes | ~90% | 21× vs. 30 min |
| 10 minutes | ~80% | 10× vs. 30 min |
| 30 minutes | ~10% | 1× (baseline) |
| 1 hour | ~5% | 0.5× |
| 24 hours | <1% | 60× less likely than 1 hour |

The takeaway is brutal: **contact potential drops by roughly 90% in the first 25 minutes.** That's not a linear curve — it's a cliff.

Velocify's study of millions of lead records added even more granularity: responding within **1 minute delivers a 391% improvement in contact rate** compared to waiting just 2 minutes. That single minute is the most valuable minute in the entire sales process.

## What That Means in Dollars

Let's translate those percentages into real money.

Using conservative, real-world assumptions for a Metro Detroit service business — 500 monthly leads with an average deal size of $5,000:

| Response Time | Qualification Rate | Qualified Deals/Month | Potential Revenue |
|---------------|-------------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Under 5 minutes | 21% | 105 | **$525,000** |
| 5–30 minutes | 9% | 45 | $225,000 |
| 30–60 minutes | 4% | 20 | $100,000 |
| 1–24 hours | 1.5% | 8 | $40,000 |
| 24+ hours | <0.5% | 2 | $10,000 |

That's a **$425,000 monthly difference** between responding within 5 minutes and responding within an hour. Annually? Well over $5 million in revenue left on the table.

For a smaller business generating 100 leads per month at $2,500 average deal size, the gap is still **$42,500 per month** — over half a million dollars annually.

## The After-Hours Blind Spot

This is where the math gets really painful.

**73% of home services calls come outside standard business hours** — evenings, weekends, holidays. And these callers have the highest intent. They have an emergency, a project deadline, or a task they're finally getting to on a Saturday.

Yet 74.1% of business calls go completely unanswered. And 85% of callers who don't reach someone won't call back.

Let's quantify that.

A contractor spending $75 per lead on Google Ads generates 100 leads per month. At a 62% missed-call rate (industry average), that's **62 lost calls × $75 = $4,650 in wasted ad spend per month.** $55,800 per year. For just one channel.

Plus the 73% after-hours volume — where competition is nearly zero and caller intent is highest. A business that answers after-hours captures leads their competitors literally never know existed.
