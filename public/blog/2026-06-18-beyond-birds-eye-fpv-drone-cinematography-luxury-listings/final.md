## What Is FPV Drone Cinematography?

FPV stands for **First Person View**. Unlike a standard camera drone, where the pilot maintains a visual line of sight with the aircraft and captures footage from a distance, an FPV drone streams live video directly to goggles or a monitor the pilot wears. The pilot sees exactly what the camera sees — as if they're flying through the space themselves.

This changes everything about how real estate footage is captured.

A standard drone (like a DJI Mavic or Phantom) excels at:

- **Static aerials:** Broad establishing shots of the property, neighborhood, and surrounding landscape.
- **Orbit shots:** Flying in a smooth circle around a structure to show its exterior from all angles.
- **High-altitude overviews:** Context shots that show lot size, proximity to amenities, and property boundaries.
- **Automated flight paths:** Pre-programmed routes that return consistent, repeatable footage.

An FPV drone adds capabilities standard drones simply cannot match:

- **Indoor walkthroughs:** Full manual control lets the pilot fly through interior spaces — rooms, hallways, staircases — capturing the spatial flow in one continuous shot.
- **Seamless indoor-to-outdoor transitions:** One uninterrupted camera pass from the living room, through the patio doors, across the deck, and into the backyard. No cuts, no edits, no loss of immersion.
- **Dynamic approach sequences:** Sweeping fly-ins from the street that pass through the front door and continue through the interior, giving buyers a visceral sense of arrival.
- **Tight obstacle navigation:** FPV drones can fly through narrow spaces — between columns, through gateways, around landscaping features — that would be impossible or dangerous for a larger camera drone.
- **Cinematic motion:** Manual flight control enables creative camera movements — rolls, flips, controlled descents, speed ramps — that produce footage indistinguishable from professional filmmaking.

The key distinction is philosophical: a standard drone captures *property photography from above*, while an FPV drone captures *the experience of being in the property*. That experiential quality is what luxury buyers are paying for.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is FPV drone cinematography for real estate?

FPV (First Person View) drone cinematography is a method of capturing property footage where the pilot wears video goggles and flies the drone as if they're moving through the space themselves. This creates one continuous, cinematic camera pass through interiors and exteriors, producing an immersive viewing experience far beyond standard drone photography.

### How is FPV drone filming different from regular drone photography?

Standard drones operate from a distance — they hover at altitude, capture broad establishing shots, and return to the pilot. FPV drones fly through the property — through rooms, down hallways, out onto patios, and across the yard. Standard drone footage is a *view of* the property. FPV footage is a *journey through* the property.

### Can FPV drones film indoor walkthroughs?

Yes — this is one of FPV's primary advantages. FPV drones are small enough (typically 250-500g with a stabilized camera) to navigate through standard doorways, up staircases, and between furniture. Standard camera drones are too large, too loud, and lack the manual control for safe indoor flight.
